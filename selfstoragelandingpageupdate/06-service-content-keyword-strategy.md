#### Section 2: The problem

**Headline:** Your website answers zero of the questions renters are asking Google.

Before a renter reserves a unit, they usually search five to fifteen times. They are comparing sizes, asking about climate control, looking up moving truck rentals, and reading about what they can and cannot store. If your website does not answer those questions, a competitor's blog or an aggregator site does, and that visitor is gone.

#### Section 3: Our content and keyword framework

**1. Keyword architecture for self-storage**
We map every page your facility needs across five content categories:

- Core service pages (one per unit type and per service you offer)
- Location pages (one per neighborhood, zip code, or nearby city you want to rank in)
- Unit size guides (5x5, 5x10, 10x10, 10x15, 10x20, 10x30, and so on)
- Move-in and moving content (checklists, truck rental pairings, packing guides)
- Informational content (tenant insurance, climate control, security, long-term storage tips)

**2. Search intent matching**
Every page is built to match what the searcher actually wants. A page targeting "10x10 storage unit" shows a renter what fits in that size, what it typically costs in your market, and a clear booking path. A page targeting "how to pack fragile items for storage" earns trust first and introduces your facility as the natural place to store those items.

**3. Local landing pages done right**
A city or neighborhood page is a real page with real content, not a thin template with the city name swapped in. Each page covers what storage in that specific area looks like, references landmarks renters recognize, and links to the nearest facility you operate.

**4. Seasonal and recurring content**
College move-in and move-out, holiday decoration storage, seasonal gear rotation, summer RV storage, winter boat storage. We build a calendar so your content is always timely.

**5. On-page optimization**
Title tags, meta descriptions, headers, image alt text, internal linking, and schema markup. Every page is built to rank from day one.

#### Section 4: The storage edge

**Headline:** A storage content plan looks nothing like an e-commerce content plan.

Self-storage content has specific high-intent patterns. Unit size guides convert. Neighborhood pages convert. Moving checklists convert. General lifestyle blog posts about decluttering rarely do. We know the difference and we only build what earns you reservations.

#### Section 6: FAQ

**Who writes the content?**
Our writers. We have a pool of writers trained on self-storage conventions, pricing language, and local market patterns. You review and approve every piece before it publishes.

**How many pieces of content do I need?**
It depends on your current coverage and competitive landscape. A typical starting program covers 12 core service and location pages plus 24 to 48 informational pieces over the first year.

**Will the content be unique to my facility?**
Yes. Every piece is written for your facility, your market, and your unit mix. We never use templated content that gets duplicated across multiple clients.

**How long does content take to rank?**
Core service and location pages typically rank within 60 to 120 days. Informational content can take 3 to 9 months depending on keyword difficulty, but once it ranks it tends to stay ranked for years.
