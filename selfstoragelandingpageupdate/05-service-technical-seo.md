#### Section 2: The problem

**Headline:** Your website is quietly losing reservations.

A self-storage website has three technical jobs: load fast, stay crawlable, and pass booking data cleanly. When any of these break, move-ins drop before you notice. The most common issues we find:

- Pages that take five seconds or longer to load on a phone, which kills mobile reservations
- Booking software that renders through JavaScript in ways Google cannot crawl, hiding your unit inventory from search results
- Duplicate pages generated automatically by your platform that dilute the ranking signals of your best pages
- A robots.txt file or meta robots tag accidentally blocking your most important pages from being indexed
- Missing or broken structured data, so Google cannot understand that you are a self-storage facility with specific services and hours

#### Section 3: Our technical SEO framework

**1. Core Web Vitals and mobile speed**
We tune images, clean up render-blocking scripts, and work with your platform to get Largest Contentful Paint under 2.5 seconds on 4G mobile. Most renters search on a phone. A fast page is a booked page.

**2. Booking software integration**
We work with the major self-storage platforms (SiteLink, storEDGE, Easy Storage Solutions, Storable, and others) to make sure your unit inventory and pricing data is crawlable and properly structured for search engines. If your booking flow uses iframes or client-side rendering, we configure server-side rendering or prerendering so Google can see your inventory.

**3. Structured data for self-storage**
We implement the full schema markup stack relevant to your facility: LocalBusiness, Service, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, and where applicable SelfStorage where it is supported. Proper schema lifts click-through rates from search results and feeds Google's knowledge of your facility.

**4. Crawling and indexing hygiene**
We audit robots.txt, sitemap.xml, canonical tags, pagination, and internal linking. We find and fix orphaned pages (pages with no internal links pointing to them), thin duplicate pages, and soft 404 errors that waste Google's crawl budget.

**5. Site architecture**
We map your pages so that each unit size, each service, and each location has a single canonical URL that earns authority. No more five near-duplicate pages splitting the ranking signals between them.
